Ongoing Projects

Our team is actively working on cutting-edge innovations that align with sustainability and advanced material science. Below is an overview of our key ongoing projects:

DMAS (Dynamic Material Adaptation System)

Objective: Developing an adaptive material system that can respond to environmental changes dynamically.

  • Integration of smart polymers and nanomaterials for self-healing and shape memory properties.
  • Potential applications in protective coatings, biomedical implants, and aerospace engineering.
  • Focus on enhancing material longevity and reducing waste through adaptability.

Barrier Layer Technology

Objective: Creating high-performance barrier coatings to improve product durability and resistance.

  • Development of hydrophobic and hydrophilic coatings for tailored applications.
  • Use of nanostructured materials for enhanced resistance against moisture, oxygen, and chemicals.
  • Applications in food packaging, electronics, and corrosion-resistant surfaces.

Sustainable Adhesive

Objective: Repurposing waste materials, including Expanded Polystyrene (EPS), into a high-performance, eco-friendly adhesive.

  • Developed through a novel chemical recycling process to reduce plastic waste.
  • Customizable formulations, including quick-dry variants for industrial and consumer use.
  • Market validation conducted with industries like Pidilite and other adhesive manufacturers.
  • Potential applications in construction, woodworking, and packaging sectors.

UF Resin (Urea-Formaldehyde Resin) Improvement

Objective: Enhancing UF resin for better performance while reducing formaldehyde emissions.

  • Researching bio-based or low-emission alternatives to improve environmental sustainability.
  • Optimizing curing properties for applications in plywood, MDF, and particleboard manufacturing.
  • Compliance with stringent emission regulations to promote sustainable industrial practices.

Para Amino Phenol (PAP) Synthesis & Optimization

Objective: Developing an efficient and sustainable method for PAP production, a key intermediate in pharmaceuticals and dyes.

  • Exploring innovative synthesis routes to improve yield and purity.
  • Evaluating green chemistry principles to reduce waste and environmental impact.
  • Target applications include paracetamol production, rubber antioxidants, and textile dyes.

At SUMACOM, we are committed to pushing the boundaries of material science and sustainability. Our projects are designed to create impactful, scalable, and industry-ready solutions.
